Building a Warm Design System for African Apps

Rakib Hasan
Jun 22, 2026 · 5 min read

When we set out to design EVINIE, we wanted a product that felt warm and human rather than cold and corporate. Most social platforms lean on stark blues and harsh whites. We chose a soft amber palette inspired by the golden hour light you see across Lagos and Dhaka rooftops at dusk.

The goal was simple: every screen should feel like an invitation, not an interface. That meant generous spacing, gentle hairlines, and a single accent colour used sparingly.

Why amber over blue?

Amber carries connotations of energy, optimism, and craft. In a region where mobile commerce is exploding, we wanted a colour that signals trust without feeling sterile. Blue is safe — amber is memorable.

We restricted the gradient to primary actions only. Buttons, active states, and brand moments glow amber, while everything else stays calm on a near-white canvas. The result is a hierarchy your thumb can read instantly.

Thumb-first from day one

Every primary action lives within reach of the thumb at the bottom of the screen. Navigation never demands a stretch. This single constraint shaped dozens of layout decisions and made the whole product feel effortless.

Design systems are never finished — they grow with the community that uses them. We can't wait to see what you build on top of EVINIE.
